# Cold Outreach Templates — Lagos & Abuja SMB Prospects

Three templates, grouped by what actually sells in motion: product-in-hand brands, wearable/styled brands, and experience-led venues. Swap the bracketed bits per company using the columns already in your sheet (Company, Description, Address/Location).

Each one is short on purpose. The goal of email one is a reply, not a sale.

---

## Template A — Product brands (Skincare, Cosmetics, Beverage, Perfumes)

**Subject line options (rotate these, don't reuse one across the whole batch):**
- A quick video idea for [Company]
- Saw [Company] on Finelib — quick thought
- 20 seconds that could sell more [product type]

**Body:**

Hi [Company] team,

I came across [Company] while looking at [skincare/cosmetics/beverage/fragrance] brands in [Lagos/Abuja], and [one specific detail from their Description, e.g. "the shea butter range caught my eye"].

I make short product motion videos, the kind that stop the scroll on Instagram and WhatsApp Status rather than another static product photo. I'd like to put one together for [Company], no cost, no obligation, just to show you what it could look like for your products.

If that's useful, reply with one or two product images and I'll turn around a short concept within a few days.

Best,
[Your name]
Dako Studios

## Sending notes

- **Batch size:** 15–20 a day max from one inbox, spread across the day rather than all at once. This list has no verifier pass yet (see note below), so smaller batches also protect your domain reputation while you watch for bounces.
- **Format:** Plain text only for the first send. No logo, no links, no attachments. Links and images are what push cold email into spam folders, especially on Gmail/Yahoo, which is the bulk of this list.
- **Personalise the bracketed detail every time.** A generic line like "I came across your brand" reads as a template and gets ignored. Pulling one real detail from the Description column takes seconds and meaningfully lifts replies.
- **Follow-up (day 4–5), only to non-repliers:**

  > Hi [Company], following up on my note below in case it got buried. Happy to put a short concept together for [Company] whenever's useful, no pressure either way.
  > [original email quoted below]

- **One more follow-up (day 10), then stop:**

  > No worries if now isn't the right time for [Company] — happy to circle back later in the year if that's better. Best, [Your name]

- **Compliance:** Include your name, Dako Studios, and a way to opt out (e.g. "reply STOP and I won't follow up again") in at least the first email. These are public business inboxes, not personal addresses, so this is standard B2B prospecting, but the opt-out keeps it clean.

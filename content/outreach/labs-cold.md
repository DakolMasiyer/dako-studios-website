# Dako Studios — labs-cold.md

The single spec any AI step (drafting, triage, follow-up) reads before acting. Plain markdown, lives at the repo root, travels with the project.

## Identity

Sender: Dakol, founder of Dako Studios (web-design arm of Dakon Enterprises).
Sending as a person, not a company — first-person, one signature, no team/agency framing in the first email.
Service: UX-led websites for SMBs and professional-services firms, delivered in 5-10 days, fully yours at handoff.

## Audience

Target: SMBs and professional-services firms based in Lagos or Abuja, plus diaspora-facing businesses serving clients in the UK, Canada, US, or Australia.
Verticals currently in scope: Real Estate & Property, Law & Professional Services, Healthcare & Clinics, Hospitality & Restaurants, Diaspora & International Services.
Why this audience: these are trust-driven, lead-driven businesses where a visitor decides whether to call, book, or wire money based on what they see in the first ten seconds. A broken or missing web presence costs them business they'll never see lost.

Disqualifiers (do not pursue or downgrade priority):
- Large, well-known brands that already run with an agency or in-house dev team.
- Businesses with a clearly modern, fast, mobile-friendly site already in place — match on actual site quality, not just "they have a website".
- Dead-end inboxes (legal@, careers@, hr@, pr@, press@) — prefer hello@/info@ on the same domain instead.

## Value

One sentence: A UX-led website that gets the booking or the enquiry instead of losing it, delivered in 5-10 days and fully yours at handoff — no monthly retainer, no waiting on us to update a price.

## Voice

Do:
- Plain text, conversational, short sentences.
- UK English spelling and idiom throughout.
- One named, checkable defect in their current web presence in every opener — pulled from an actual visit to their site or socials, never generic ("broken contact form", "no mobile version", "no website at all" — not "your online presence could be improved").
- Read like one founder writing to another, not a template.

Don't:
- No unnecessary hyphenation — let sentences run naturally rather than chaining hyphenated phrases.
- No links, logos, or attachments in the first email (deliverability).
- No fake urgency ("only 2 spots left", "offer ends tonight").
- No guarantees about lead or sales outcomes — the offer is a mockup, not a promise of results.
- No overly formal or corporate register — this is SMB-to-SMB, not enterprise sales.

## Sequence

1. **Opener (day 0):** Template by niche (see lead research). One named defect, one line on why it costs them business, one clear ask: a quick free mockup of what a modern site would look like for them.
2. **Bump (day 4–5), non-repliers only:** Short, low-pressure nudge referencing the original email. No new pitch.
3. **Breakup (day 10), still no reply:** One line, gives them an easy out, leaves the door open for later in the year. Then stop — no further automated contact.

## Objections

Common pushback and the preferred angle to answer with (a human still writes the actual reply, this is the angle to draft from):

- **"What does this cost?"** → Don't lead with a price list. Reply that the first mockup is free so they can see the work before any conversation about cost, and ask what pages or features matter most to them.
- **"We already have a website."** → Acknowledge it, then point to the specific named defect already raised (broken form, slow load, no mobile view) — the free mockup is a no-risk way to see what a rebuild would look like, alongside what they have now, not a hard pitch to replace it.
- **"Not sure we need a new site."** → Point to where their actual customers are deciding to contact them or not — mobile visitors, Google searches, referrals checking credibility — and that a slow or dated site is costing enquiries they'll never see, more than it's an aesthetic preference.
- **"Send more info first."** → Keep it light — a couple of lines on how the free mockup works (we build it, you look at it, no obligation), then ask for their domain or a few brand details to get started, rather than a long pitch deck.

## Banned

Phrases that should never appear in outreach copy:
- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "Just circling back"
- "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary" / "Disrupt"
- "Guaranteed results" or any sales-outcome promise
- Any claimed client name, testimonial, or number not actually verified
- Fake security or SEO scare tactics ("your site is at risk", "Google is about to penalise you", "hackers target sites like yours") — the defect cited must be real and checkable, not invented to scare

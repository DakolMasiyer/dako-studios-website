## Question 3 — Persona A: SME / Business Owner (Labs + Brand + Motion)

```
Who they are: Business owner, often with a partner or office manager involved in the decision. Real estate agents, law firms, retail/product brands, diaspora-run businesses.
Geography: Nigeria-based and Nigerian diaspora — roughly equal mix.
What they want: A web/brand presence that actually generates leads and looks credible to prospects who find them.
What stops them today: Two pains, roughly equal weight — (1) losing leads to competitors who already have a web presence, and (2) losing trust when prospects do find them via an outdated or Facebook-only page.
What language they respond to: Direct, plain-spoken, numbers over adjectives.
Trust signals (in priority order):
  1. Seeing a site/build for a business like theirs (same vertical or business type)
  2. Named/recognizable proof points (film campaign numbers, known client names — even outside their own industry)
  3. Clear speed and price upfront
```

## Question 6 — Core Differentiators (Exactly 3)

```
Differentiator 1: UX-led design thinking, not just visual assembly. A founder-level UX background means every build starts from why a visitor is on the page and what needs to happen before they leave — not just aesthetic assembly. Result: sites that convert, not just look good.

Differentiator 2: The handoff model. Built on Framer with a client-editable CMS — clients can update copy, prices, and images after launch without a developer, a retainer, or touching code. Independence within a professionally designed container — distinct from freelancers who disappear post-launch, agencies that charge per edit, or DIY tools that give too much rope with no structure.

Differentiator 3: One relationship, multiple capabilities. Labs, Brand, Motion, and Film under one roof — a client who needs a website today can get a brand refresh or product video later without switching vendors. Multi-arm capability at boutique price with founder-level attention — a solo freelancer can't offer the breadth, and a traditional agency charges enterprise rates for the same thing.
```

## Question 9 — Brand Voice (5 Principles)

```
Principle 1 (directness): We talk like a business owner, not a vendor pitching a deck — short sentences, no "leverage synergies," just what we'll build and why it'll work.

Principle 2 (specificity): Numbers over adjectives — "#1 on Prime Video Nigeria in 5 days" beats "high-impact campaign." If we don't have a number yet, we say what we're building toward instead of inflating what exists.

Principle 3 (transparency / building in public): We show the actual process — wireframes, before/afters, the founder's own UX background — and we're upfront when proof comes from founder-led work that predates the Dako Studios brand.

Principle 4 (confidence): We don't hedge. "Your site is built to convert" not "we hope this helps your business." The work backs it up.

Principle 5 (what you never do): Never claim results we can't attribute, never talk down to a small business owner like they don't understand their own market, never bury the pitch in agency jargon.
```

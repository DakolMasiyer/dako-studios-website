## 1. The Problem We're Solving

Femi and businesses like him need professional creative work — websites, brand identity, motion content — but freelancers are unreliable, traditional agencies are priced for enterprise, and DIY tools (Wix/Canva) produce generic results that don't convert.

The gap is structural, not budget or ambition. SMEs across Nigeria and the diaspora are stuck between two bad options: expensive traditional agencies, or DIY tools that produce a site or brand that doesn't generate leads or earn trust.

Dako Studios is the studio that closes that gap — multiple specialized arms (Labs, Brand, Motion, Film, Academy) under one roof, so clients get senior-level, UX-led creative work at a price and speed that fits their stage, and keep full ownership of it after launch.

## 4. Content Strategy

### Four-Phase Arc

| Phase | Month | Priority shift | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prove the model | Month 1 | Education 35%, Proof 25%, BTS 20% — weighted to Labs | Build awareness, establish credibility for Labs |
| Build trust | Month 2 | Education 30%, Proof 30%, BTS 20% — Brand/Motion content phased in | Deepen engagement, grow Labs pipeline + open Brand/Motion |
| Demonstrate scale | Month 3 | Proof 35%, BTS 25%, Education 20% — Film phased in | Convert — proposals, closed clients across arms |
| Compound | Month 4+ | Proof 40%, Culture 20%, BTS 20% | Scale via referral and repeat-client flywheel |

*Culture (15%) and Industry (5%) hold constant across all phases. Academy content (Zainab) runs in parallel at a steady cadence throughout — it already has live traction and shouldn't be deprioritised.*

### The Five Content Pillars

**1. Education (20–35%)**
Teach Femi what a real website/brand/motion asset should do for his business — most SME owners don't know why a Facebook page can't replace a website, what actually makes a site convert, or what a professional handoff should look like. Dako Studios becomes the trusted educator before the vendor.
*Format: Carousels, threads, blog posts*

**2. Proof (25–40%)**
Document every project milestone and portfolio piece publicly: before/after reveals, the First Features and Double Bill film campaign numbers (#1 charts, 1M+ reach, 43% open rate), the Sporton brand case study, the Kiichen UX case study. Specific over vague — client type, turnaround time, named outcome.
*Format: Case study posts, before/after, outcome stories*

**3. Behind the Scenes (20–25%)**
Show the studio's process in real time — a Framer build in progress, a UX wireframing session, a founder day-in-the-life across 5 arms. Building in public as brand strategy.
*Format: Short video, LinkedIn posts, stories*

**4. Culture (15%)**
Position Dako Studios as part of the Nigerian/African creative-tech scene, building senior-level creative work for and by the local market — not an outsider vendor. Own the angle that professional creative work isn't only for big budgets.
*Format: Profile posts, community moments, industry commentary*

**5. Industry (5%)**
Signals from African SME digitization and design/dev trends — what's changing for small businesses online, platform shifts relevant to Ngozi's world (streaming, distribution). Positions Dako Studios as an informed insider.
*Format: LinkedIn threads, commentary posts*

## 6. Outreach System

One outreach funnel structure applies to every persona: **Awareness → Lead → Proposal → Client → Referral.** Run it once per persona group, weighted by phase priority (Labs first).

### Femi Track — Active Now

| Sequence | Trigger | Touches | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold outreach | Identified via no-website/outdated-site lead list (Run 1-15 pipeline) | 3 messages over 7 days | Email / DM | Discovery call booked |
| Proposal | Discovery call attended | 1 proposal + 2 follow-ups over 7 days | Email | Client closed |
| Re-engagement | No reply after cold message 3, or proposal not accepted | 1x/month | Email | Re-engage, keep warm |

**Cold sequence arc:**
Day 0 intro (who we are, what we do, why now) → Day 3 education (why a Facebook page isn't enough) → Day 7 social proof (named campaign numbers + call-booking CTA)

**Proposal arc:**
Day 0 proposal sent → Day 3 check-in → Day 7 final follow-up before moving to re-engagement

### Ngozi Track — Phase 3+

| Sequence | Trigger | Touches | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold outreach | LinkedIn connection / industry referral | 3 messages over 7 days | LinkedIn / Email | Discovery call booked |
| Proposal | Call attended | 2 touches over 5 days | Email | Project closed |

### Zainab Track — Parallel, Always On

| Sequence | Trigger | Touches | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enrollment funnel | Lands on learn.dako.studio or sees content | Ongoing content + 1 nurture sequence | Site / Email | Enrolled |

## 7. Brand Voice

- **Directness:** We talk like a business owner, not a vendor pitching a deck — short sentences, no "leverage synergies," just what we'll build and why it'll work.
- **Specificity:** Numbers over adjectives — "#1 on Prime Video Nigeria in 5 days" beats "high-impact campaign." If we don't have a number yet, we say what we're building toward instead of inflating what exists.
- **Transparency / building in public:** We show the actual process — wireframes, before/afters, the founder's own UX background — and we're upfront when proof comes from founder-led work that predates the Dako Studios brand.
- **Confidence:** We don't hedge. "Your site is built to convert" not "we hope this helps your business." The work backs it up.
- **What we never do:** Never claim results we can't attribute, never talk down to a small business owner like they don't understand their own market, never bury the pitch in agency jargon.

## 11. Objection Handling Summary

*(Full detail in `08_OBJECTION_HANDLING_TEMPLATE.md`)*

1. "This is too expensive for my business" → Reframe cost vs. cost of staying invisible/outdated; show ROI comparable

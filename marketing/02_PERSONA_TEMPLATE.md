## Persona A — Femi (SME / Business Owner)

> Primary buyer for **Labs, Brand, and Motion**. This is the current revenue priority persona — Labs is the active sprint.

### Who They Are
```
Role / title: Business owner — often with a partner or office manager involved in the decision
Business type: Real estate agent, law firm, retail/product brand, diaspora-run business
Geography: Nigeria-based and Nigerian diaspora — roughly equal mix
Age range: 30–55
Tools they use today: Facebook page, WhatsApp Business, possibly an outdated/template website
Where they spend time online: Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, LinkedIn (if professional services)
```

### What They Want
```
Primary goal: A web/brand presence that generates real leads and looks credible to prospects who find them
Secondary goal: Spend less time managing their online presence themselves, without losing control of it
What success looks like to them in 3 months: A site that's live, looks professional next to competitors, and is already producing inbound enquiries
```

### What Blocks Them
```
Pain 1 (biggest, tied): Losing leads to competitors who already have a working web presence
Pain 2 (biggest, tied): Losing trust when prospects do find them — outdated or Facebook-only page reads as "not a real business"
Pain 3: Past bad experience with a freelancer or DIY tool that didn't deliver
What they've tried before and why it didn't work: DIY builders (Wix/Canva) that produced generic results, or a freelancer who disappeared post-launch
```

### How They Communicate
```
Language style: Direct, plain-spoken, informal — talks like a peer running a business, not a corporate buyer
Responds to: Numbers over adjectives, seeing a build for a business like theirs first
Turned off by: Agency jargon, vague promises, slow or unclear pricing
Trust signals that work on them (in priority order):
  1. Seeing a site/build for a business like theirs (same vertical or business type)
  2. Named/recognizable proof points (film campaign numbers, known client names — even outside their industry)
  3. Clear speed and price upfront
```

### Key Messages for This Persona
```
Message 1: Your site is built to convert, not just to look good
Message 2: Once it's live, it's yours to run — no developer, no retainer, no waiting on us for a price change
Message 3: We're not a template, not a freelancer who vanishes, not an enterprise agency you can't afford
```

### Content That Works for This Persona
```
Best formats: Before/after reveals, case studies by vertical (real estate, law, retail), short process videos
Best platforms: Instagram, WhatsApp, Facebook, TikTok (short-form BTS/before-after)
Best posting times: Tue–Thu is the strongest window across platforms. Instagram peaks 7-9pm WAT; TikTok peaks 2-6pm; LinkedIn (if reaching the law/professional-services segment) peaks 9-11am Wed/Thu. Test WAT vs. US/UK/EU timezones separately given the Nigeria + diaspora split — don't assume one schedule fits both audiences.
```

## Persona B — Ngozi (Film / Entertainment Client)

> Primary buyer for **Film**. Secondary priority — phased in after the Labs pipeline stabilizes.

### Who They Are
```
Role / title: Marketing lead / principal at a production company or studio (e.g. Natives Filmworks) — commissions marketing directly for their own film slate, not an individual freelance filmmaker
Production type: Feature films and theatrical/streaming releases (Amazon Prime Video Nigeria)
Geography: Nigeria (Nollywood), with global streaming distribution
Tools they use today: PR agencies (sometimes more than one per release), in-house social channels
Where they spend time online: LinkedIn, Twitter/X, industry circles
```

### What They Want
```
Primary goal: Reach the right target audience precisely
Secondary goal: Translate that reach into measurable outcomes — box office or streaming chart performance
What success looks like to them on a campaign: Hitting #1 (or top chart positions) on a streaming platform within days of release — like the Double Bill campaign hitting #1 and #2 on Prime Video Nigeria in 5 days
```

### What Blocks Them
```
Pain 1 (biggest): Hard to know if marketing spend is reaching the audience that actually converts to viewers/ticket buyers, vs. just generating impressions
Pain 2: Coordinating multiple agencies/channels around a single release without a unified strategy
What they've tried before and its limitations: Working with PR agencies in isolation, without a coordinated content/SEO/email/paid strategy tying everything to one theme
```

### How They Communicate
```
Language style: Results-first, chart/numbers-driven, peer-level (studio to studio)
Responds to: Documented chart outcomes, engagement and reach numbers, coordinated campaign case studies
Trust signals that work on them: Named campaign results (#1 Nigeria Top Movies chart, #1/#2 Prime Video Nigeria), specific reach/engagement stats (1M+ Meta reach, 43% email open rate)
```

### Key Messages for This Persona
```
Message 1: We don't just generate impressions — we reach the audience that converts to viewers
Message 2: One coordinated strategy across content, SEO, email, and paid — not a patchwork of disconnected agencies
```

### Best Channels to Reach Them
```
Primary channel: LinkedIn
Secondary channel: Direct industry relationships / referral
Events / communities: Nigerian film industry circles, Nollywood production networks
```

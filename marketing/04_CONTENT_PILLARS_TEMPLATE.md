## Pillar 1 — Education

**What it is:**
Teach Femi what a real website or brand asset should do for his business *before* pitching him anything. Most SME owners don't know why a Facebook page can't replace a website, what actually makes a site convert, or what a proper handoff looks like. This pillar makes Dako Studios the trusted educator first, vendor second.

**Persona:** Femi (primary), Zainab (secondary)
**Phase weight:** 35% Month 1 → 20% Month 4+
**Formats:** Carousels, threads, short explainer video, blog posts

**Guardrails:**
- No agency jargon — if the term isn't in the Jargon Buster, don't use it
- No condescension — Femi understands his business better than we do; we're explaining *our world* to him, not his world back to him
- No vague tips — every post must have one actionable takeaway
- Never manufacture urgency ("limited time!") — specificity creates urgency on its own

---

### Post Ideas — Education

**IG-01** `[IG — Carousel, 6 slides]`
**"5 things your website should do before a visitor scrolls past the first screen"**
Slide 1: Hook — "Most SME websites lose the visitor in under 8 seconds. Here's why."
Slides 2–6: Clear headline → One visible CTA → Trust signal (testimonial/client logo) → Mobile-first layout → Fast load. Each slide = one point, one sentence, one visual.
CTA: "Which of these is your site missing? Drop a 🔴 below."

---

**"Facebook page vs. website — this is not the same thing"**
Slide 1: "If your only online presence is a Facebook page, you're invisible to 60% of people looking for you."

CTA: "Save this. Share it with someone still running their business from a Facebook page."

---

**IG-03** `[IG — Single graphic]`
**"What a website handoff should actually look like"**
Visual: Two columns — "What most freelancers give you" vs. "What a proper handoff includes."
Left: "A link and a password." Right: "Editable CMS, training, domain ownership, documented structure."
CTA: "You built the business. The site should belong to you too."

## Pillar 2 — Proof

**What it is:**
Document every project milestone, portfolio piece, and outcome publicly. Specificity is the entire point — client type, turnaround time, named result. Vague claims ("we deliver great work") are invisible. Named outcomes ("Double Bill: #1 + #2 on Prime Video Nigeria within 5 days") earn trust.

**Persona:** Femi (primary), Ngozi (secondary)
**Phase weight:** 25% Month 1 → 40% Month 4+
**Formats:** Case study posts, before/after reveals, outcome stories, milestone posts

**Guardrails:**
- Never claim a result that can't be attributed — if it predates the Dako Studios brand, say "Founder-led work, previously at [context]"
- Specific over vague, always — "#1 on Prime Video Nigeria in 5 days" not "high-impact film campaign"
- Before/afters require real before (screenshot, not a mockup of a bad site)
- [PENDING] items stay pending until the number is real — do not estimate or project

## Pillar 3 — Behind the Scenes

**What it is:**

A wireframing session, a Framer build in progress, a founder day across 5 arms — all of it signals that the work is real, the process is structured, and the person behind it knows what they're doing.

**Persona:** Femi (process credibility), Zainab (Academy aspiration)
**Phase weight:** 20% throughout all phases
**Formats:** Short video, TikTok, Stories, LinkedIn posts

**Guardrails:**
- Show real work — not staged "aesthetic" desk shots
- If showing client work in progress, confirm client permission first
- Keep it process-focused, not personal-brand-focused — the studio is the subject, not the founder's lifestyle

## Pillar 4 — Culture

**What it is:**
Position Dako Studios as part of the Nigerian and African creative-tech scene — building senior-level work for and by the local market. Own the angle that professional creative output isn't reserved for large budgets or Western agencies. This pillar builds belonging, not just awareness.

**Persona:** Femi (belonging + trust), Zainab (aspiration), Ngozi (peer signal)
**Phase weight:** 15% throughout all phases
**Formats:** Profile posts, community moments, industry commentary

**Guardrails:**
- Never romanticise struggle or poverty — the angle is capability and ambition, not overcoming adversity
- No performative "Africa rising" language — specific and grounded always
- Don't punch at competitors by name
